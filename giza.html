Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is one of three pyramids in the Giza Pyramid Complex. Based on a mark in an interior chamber naming the work gang and a reference to the Fourth Dynasty Egyptian pharaoh Khufu, some Egyptologists believe that the pyramid was thus built as a tomb, taking 10 to 20 years to build. The Great Pyramid has three known chambers in it, however the lowest chamber is unfinished.

The Great Pyramid is made of 2.3 million blocks, carried across the river from nearby quarries.

It is not yet known how the pyramids were built, but they appear to have been built by skilled Egyptian workers
